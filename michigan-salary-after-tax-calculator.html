Michigan State Income Tax Overview (2026)

Michigan applies a flat state income tax rate to taxable income. Unlike progressive systems used in some states, Michigan taxes all eligible income at a single percentage rate. This means your effective state tax depends mainly on your total taxable income rather than bracket tiers.

In addition to federal income tax, employees in Michigan also contribute to Social Security and Medicare through payroll taxes.

Example Take-Home Pay in Michigan

For example, if you earn $70,000 per year in Michigan as a single filer, your total tax obligation includes federal income tax plus Michigan's flat state tax. After subtracting estimated taxes, your annual and monthly take-home pay can be calculated using this tool.

Federal vs State Taxes in Michigan

Federal income tax rates are progressive and apply nationwide. Michigan, however, uses a flat state tax structure. Understanding the difference between federal and state taxation helps you better estimate your net income and plan your finances accordingly.
