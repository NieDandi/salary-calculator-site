How Income Tax Works in California

In addition to federal income tax, California residents pay state income tax. The rate increases based on your taxable income. High earners may also be subject to additional surcharges.

Example: $50,000 Salary in California

If you earn $50,000 per year in California and file as Single:

  • Federal tax will apply based on IRS brackets
  • California state tax will be deducted
  • Social Security and Medicare (FICA) are also deducted

Your estimated take-home pay may be approximately $38,000–$40,000 depending on deductions.

Example: $100,000 Salary in California

For a $100,000 annual salary, total combined federal and state tax can exceed 30%. California’s progressive tax structure significantly impacts higher income earners.
